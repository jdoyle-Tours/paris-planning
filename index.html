Sample stops from a
recent itinerary

Built for a couple — art history focus, serious food interest, first trip to Paris. Pace: unhurried.

Day 1 — Morning
Sainte-Chapelle, before the crowds
Arrive at 9am. The lower chapel first — then the upper. Stand at the east end when the morning light hits the rose window. Allow 45 minutes minimum.
Day 2 — Afternoon
The Medici Fountain, Luxembourg
Sit on the stone edge of the canal with something from the Rue de Buci market. This is the best half-hour available to you on the Left Bank.
Day 3 — Evening
Dinner at a specific table
Not just a restaurant. A specific restaurant chosen for this itinerary, with ordering guidance, wine recommendations, and what to skip.
Day 5 — Full Day
The Marais, properly
The Musée Carnavalet for context. The Place des Vosges at noon. The Jewish quarter. Jacques Genin for chocolate. Not the version you find in a guidebook.

I've been traveling to Paris for years — not as a tourist, but as someone genuinely interested in the city's layers: its medieval street plans, its nineteenth-century painting, its postwar intellectual life, its extraordinary food culture.
